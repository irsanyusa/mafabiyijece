Whats the deal with a diphthong?

In English, we learn that the vowels are A, E, I, O, U (and sometimes Y!). That seems like the end of the story, but there is more. When vowels connect or become diphthongs, they have special pronunciation rules. Diphthongs get our mouths moving to make new vowel sounds. It’s not just English; lots of languages have diphthongs too.  Today, we will learn all about diphthongs and how to read them properly.
